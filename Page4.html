My design voice was a big part of my unit 6 project, it being a group project we all did it in our own styles and edited it together, as well as audio I had to voice over with my own words and that made me show a vulnerability that I do not usually offer in group projects. This is very prominent in my unit 6 brief 2 Time and Movement project titled ‘Impermanent intricacies’. This brief was to create a short film based on four different headings; Possibility, Threshold, Presence and Impermanence. I chose to combine Threshold and possibility which were; A minute as possibility: 60 seconds, can change the course of a day A minute as threshold: The turning point where something new begins We were tasked to work directly on film stock, whether that be clear film, inked out or found film that was up to us. I chose to work on 35mm black inked out film and scratch into it. I worked on about 12 frames per second. The concept was watching a butterfly hatching from its chrysalis and flying around for a few frames and then coming to its end by a very hungry frog! The logline was ‘Because in the end if something so delicate can survive in the turbulent winds you can survive the day or not!’ This project was surrounded by my emotional state at that period of time, I was not sure of anything I was creating and felt like creating something that felt like how I felt at the time would help me get me out of my head. The audio played a MASSIVE part of this project as it helped the story come across how I intended it too. As much as it was sad that the butterfly came to its demise, the frog got fed and meant it could go on for another day. There is always a light side of the tunnel as nature shows us. Some pieces that I took inspiration from where; Black Eyed Dog, how the creator describes it; ‘Alone but for his dog, a man is consumed by obsession with a mysterious creature he encountered in the woods as a child.’ It's an eerily made claymation stop motion film created by Alessandro Cino Zolfanelli that confronts the feelings of mental tiredness and depression, as ‘black eyed dog’ is a known metaphor for the heavy, isolating feeling that depression brings, it follows you, drains your energy and makes it hard to connect, the dog is a presence that won't leave. As well as, Mothlight by Stan Brakhage; his explanation ‘Here is a film that I made out of a deep grief. The grief is my business in a way, but the grief was helpful in squeezing the little film out of me, that I said "these crazy moths are flying into the candlelight, and burning themselves to death, and that's what's happening to me. I don't have enough money to make these films, and ... I'm not feeding my children properly, because of these damn films, you know. And I'm burning up here ... What can I do?" I'm feeling the full horror of some kind of immolation, in a way.’ I used these both for more metaphorical references and Brakheage’s for more visual references as well. Nature can be used in many, many different ways and I felt it fit best for the brief and what I wanted to create to respond to the brief.
